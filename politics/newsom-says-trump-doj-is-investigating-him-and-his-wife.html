California Gov. Gavin Newsom says Trump’s Justice Department is investigating him and his wife

Democratic California Gov. Gavin Newsom accused the Justice Department of launching a politically-motivated investigation into him and his wife, saying the Trump administration was targeting him as he weighs a presidential bid.

California Gov. Gavin Newsom says Trump’s Justice Department is investigating him and his wife

Paul L. Mayer
16 June 2026

SACRAMENTO, Calif — Democratic California Gov. Gavin Newsom on Monday accused the Justice Department of opening a politically motivated investigation into him and his wife, saying the Trump administration was going after him as he considers a presidential run.

Newsom, a long-time political enemy of the Republican president, claimed in a video posted on X that federal investigators have knocked on the doors of his friends and former colleagues and requested for records. Newsom did not detail the scope of the examination, but his office claimed the inquiry appears to have recently expanded to “increasingly personal matters concerning the Governor’s family and professional circle.”

“Donald Trump isn’t just coming after me because of my mean tweets,” Newsom added, noting his social media use mocking Trump. “He’s coming after me because I’m thinking about running for president, because he hates that I’ve called him out consistently over and over again for his lies and deceit.”

It was not immediately obvious what the full scope of any Justice Department probes involving Newsom was. But the disclosures were certain to fuel criticism from critics that the Trump administration is weaponizing the law enforcement agency to pursue the president’s political foes.

Someone close to the situation disputed there is an investigation of the governor, per se, but said there are a number of federal examinations into persons around him, including one involving his wife’s taxes. That inquiry was opened last year, and the decision to launch it was not made with the involvement of political leadership in Washington, said the person, who spoke on condition of anonymity because they were not authorized to speak publicly about current investigations.

The report also looks into Newsom’s former chief of staff, Dana Williamson, who has been accused on federal charges claiming she was part of a plan to steal campaign money from former federal Health Secretary Xavier Becerra. Williamson pled guilty in May to charges of conspiracy to conduct bank fraud. The probe, initiated by President Joe Biden’s Justice Department, has grown to involve additional staff, the source familiar with the matter said.

Newsom has not been accused of any wrongdoing in connection with Williamson’s case.

The Justice Department declined comment Monday. Acting Attorney General Todd Blanche declined to address questions about Newsom during a brief photo session with Senate Judiciary Committee Chairman Chuck Grassley, R-Iowa, on Capitol Hill Monday afternoon. Blanche was to meet with Grassley to discuss his nomination to be attorney general.

Trump’s Justice Department targets his detractors They are the latest Justice Department probes aimed at one of Trump’s political rivals.

The Justice Department has also launched investigations into or prosecuted former FBI Director James Comey, New York Attorney General Letitia James, former CIA director John Brennan and former Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell, among other perceived political enemies of the president.

“Anyone who has taken on Donald Trump, one by one, has found themselves on his hit list, and today I proudly join that list,” Newsom stated in the video.

Newsom's office says investigators have subpoenaed records and contacted groups and persons tied to the governor and his wife. The governor’s administration charged the Justice Department was “looking for a crime that doesn’t exist.”

Newsom’s office said it found out last week that federal investigators had intensified their inquiry and were asking questions of people associated with the governor and his wife about their businesses, finances and personal lives. His office said it submitted a public records request Monday for messages to or from Justice Department leaders that reference Newsom or his wife during Trump’s second term.

“This investigation shows that Trump is unfit for office,” said the governor’s wife, Jennifer Siebel Newsom.

“There are clearly no boundaries to what Donald Trump will do to get his way or to fight those who get in his way,” she added in a statement.

The president has gone after the Democrat-dominated state repeatedly during his second term including by restricting a flagship plan to cut planet-warning pollution from autos, suspending funds for wildfire recovery and suing over state regulations favoring transgender student-athletes. The state has sued the federal government dozens of times in response.

Newsom also spearheaded a charge to resist a Trump-backed initiative in Texas to redraw congressional lines to favor Republicans by backing a successful redistricting bill in California aimed at giving Democrats five more House seats.
