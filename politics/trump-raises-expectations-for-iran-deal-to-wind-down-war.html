Trump is raising expectations that this time he really will close deal with Iran to wind down war

Paul L. Mayer
12 June 2026

WASHINGTON — President Donald Trump said Friday that the leaders of Russia and Ukraine have agreed to his call for a three-day ceasefire and prisoner exchange, adding that a pause in hostilities might be the “beginning of the end” of the lengthy war between them.

The World Cup is coming to the U.S. for the first time in 32 years after Trump pushed himself into securing the proposal to co-host the soccer event during his first term. A UFC fight night is scheduled for Sunday, his 80th birthday, and is anticipated to draw thousands to the White House grounds for the celebration. He’s jetting out to the G7 summit in the French Alps for talks with a few international leaders he’s been beefing with over war and tariffs, hours after the final bout.

But Trump raised the ante even more for the coming days when he said Thursday that the U.S. and Iran may be able to reach agreement this weekend on a deal that would chart a pathway to terminate the three-month-old battle that has been widely unpopular with Americans and has unsettled global oil markets. He said he plans to send Vice President JD Vance to attend the signing of the deal.

Trump has often declared in recent weeks that he’s close to an agreement without a deal actually materializing. After Trump’s remarks, a spokesman for Iran’s Foreign Ministry informed state television that mediators were working but nothing had been decided to settle the situation.

But Trump is saying it may be different this time.

The breakthrough comes after he promised to intensify the fight with more aggressive bombardment of Iran and by seizing control of Iran’s oil industry by conquering Iran’s crucial Kharg Island oil facility. The president’s threats came after back-and-forth strikes this week that had left a temporary ceasefire agreed to in early April all but worthless.

“They’ve taken a pounding like very few people could take,” Trump remarked in an Oval Office meeting with reporters as he explained why he was certain that, this time, a deal will come through. And they want to do the transaction much more than I do.

Trump offered few specifics of the settlement he says is coming together but told reporters he believes Iranian supreme leader Ayatollah Mojtaba Khamenei, who is thought to have been injured on the first day of the war and has not been seen in public since, is prepared to sign off on the deal.

Trump calls the deal "very strong" but says it is "still a little conceptual." He claims it would ensure Iran would never be able to create a nuclear weapon.

Trump’s stepped up threats are meant to provide an off-ramp The conflict between the two countries has escalated in the past week. Ali Vaez, Iran director at the International Crisis Group, said Trump’s threat to escalate U.S. military action was partly intended to show the hawkish wing of his political base that he was ready to play “hardball” with the Iranians if they did not strike a deal soon.

Trump vowed in March that he would attack Iranian facilities and deploy US troops on Kharg Island before retreating. The two nations reached the temporary ceasefire.

Almost as soon as he raised the subject anew on social media Thursday, Trump appeared to back off. He spoke on a morning broadcast on Fox News Channel and asked if Americans had the “stomach” for a choice that would necessitate putting U.S. troops in harm’s path.

Hours later, Trump said he had decided to cancel orders for “very hard” strikes on Iran and said a solution was close.

“Even as Trump was tweeting on Thursday about escalating strikes, mediators from Pakistan, Turkey and Qatar were making progress in their talks with Iran,” Vaez said.

Meanwhile, Iran may also have changed the equation for Trump with its decision last weekend to attack Israel directly for the first time since the ceasefire after Israeli troops carried out combat operations on Iranian-backed Hezbollah fighters in Lebanon.

The decision signified Israel could no longer strike Lebanon without expecting a substantial response, and simultaneously increased the cost for the U.S. to keep its promise to help protect Israel.

“It really does seem to me that Trump wants to put an end to this, but his real challenge is that he’s looking for a victory lap and an exit ramp and those two things are not necessarily compatible,” Vaez said.

Trump whines on war story From the first weeks of the war, Trump had been boasting that he had already won the war, that the Islamic Republic’s leadership had been largely slain in the airstrikes and that the Iranian navy and air force had been seriously crippled.

But Iran still effectively keeps the Strait of Hormuz closed, choking a waterway through which about 20% of the world’s oil supply flowed before the war, and has yet to agree to restart negotiations with the U.S. over its concerns about Iran’s nuclear program, the main reason Trump and Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu gave to justify launching the war.

But the true problem, Trump muttered Thursday, was mostly a public relations concern.

They can fly the white flag of surrender. We submit, we surrender, we are finished, we have had enough. “The greatest power in the United States, thank God,” Trump said Fox News. They could say it loud and clear. “And the fake news would say it was a great victory for Iran.”

Rep. Michael McCaul, R-Texas, a former chairman of the House Foreign Affairs Committee, said Trump has grown frustrated with Iran and the renewed strikes and threats on Kharg Island and Iran’s energy sector were meant to bring the negotiations back to the “right place.”

Polls reveal the battle is overwhelmingly unpopular with Americans. “They want to try to drag this out as long as they can because they see that as being to their benefit closer to the midterm elections in November,” McCaul added.

conflict will be high on agenda at next week’s G7 Deal or no deal, the conflict will loom big during next week’s meetings at the Group of Seven summit in bucolic Évian-les-Bains, France.

Trump has often lambasted some of the group leaders — British Prime Minister Keir Starmer, French President Emmanuel Macron, Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni and German Chancellor Friedrich Merz — for not responding to his requests for support for the U.S. and Israeli military effort.

The four leaders have also infuriated Trump by questioning how he’s gone about executing the war and his lack of engagement with friends before entering into a fight that’s affected the global economy as oil prices have climbed.

But Trump said he was hopeful of getting a deal before his meetings with leaders in France.

“The strait will open officially as soon as we sign, which could be soon, very soon — perhaps over the weekend in Europe,” Trump added.
